Art

Pop Art

As we explored the history of Pop Art and its many forms, we emulated a “Warhol” style collage by creating a canvas on which we placed selfies- iconic symbols of our current pop culture- that were altered with bright, colorful filters.

Icon

For this project, we were to study iconic photographs and celebrity figures and choose one figure to create an illustration of. Using Illustrator of the first time, we utilized this photo as the basis of our work, creating layers of illustrations using tracing and painting tools, in order to create an original illustration of this celebrity photo.

Typography

For this project, we were to observe our local environment, searching for letters of the alphabet -in either representational or abstract form- take photos of several different samples of these letters, and then use the specific ones that would spell out our names.

Goldsworthy

For this project, we looked at iconic ephemeral artist Andy Goldsworthy whose nature-inspired and nature-based installations and interruptions are commentaries on the very landscape it is created in. We were to create an interruption into nature, using only natural materials, and photograph that installation in a pleasing way to document the art.

Poetry

In this project, we were to select a line of poetry or a song lyric, and communicate the tone of that stanza with imagery that we artistically manipulated in Photoshop, along with the lyrics. The lyrics were to be typed in a font that also mirrored the tone of the poem/song lyrics. We had to be sure that the lyrics did not take away from the imagery, wer visible using high contrast, and that they were aesthetically placed.

Broobs

Ruby Marquez, usually going by the moniker “Broobs” is a Chicanx visual artist known for their political posters. They were raised in Montebello, California, and are currently living in the Bay Area. Their photo collages are socially and politically focused in subject matter and the collage-style emulates the artwork on prayer candles used in Hispanic/Latin/Chicano cultures. We were to honor the style of Broobs photo collage by choosing an iconic person and layering the collage with greenery, floral and symbols of the icon’s profession, creating a “halo” around the figure, just as Broobs would.
